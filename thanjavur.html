About Thanjavur

Thanjavur - The Rice Bowl Of TamilNadu

Thanjavur is a beautiful place in South India that is known for its historical importance and cultural significance. Some of the main reasons why this city is called a cultural heritage are its Tanjore paintings, textiles and saris, Carnatic music, and handicrafts. Alongside this, the city is home to some of the famous architectural marvels that reflect the rich heritage of the place. The list of places to visit in Thanjavur is endless, and taking a tour of these will give a glimpse of the cherished history of dynasties that have ruled here.

Popular Places In Thanjavur

Here is a list of some of the best places to visit in Thanjavur which will definitely take you to a different dimension and let you experience a time like never before.

BRIHADEESWARAR TEMPLE

A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Thanjavur, the Brihadeeswarar Temple is a Chola architectural masterpiece. The premises have a temple tower called "Vimana," which is almost 66 meters long. The round apex structure is believed to have been built out of a carving on a single stone. Upon entry, you are greeted with the huge Nandi figurine, almost 13 feet in height. Back in 2010, it completed 1,000 years of being one of the top Thanjavur tourist places.

GANGAIKONDA CHOLAPURAM

This place is an architectural beauty that was built under the reign of the Chola Empire, which is considered to be one of the greatest empires of India. Gangaikonda Cholapuram was once the capital city of the Chola empire for around two centuries. This is one of the magnificent architectures of the past and holds importance in the history of Thanjavur. Built by Chola King Rajendra to commemorate his victory in the Pala Dynasty. In the present day, this city has turned into a small village where Lord Shiva Temple is a symbol of grandeur, which makes it one of the most ideal places to visit in Thanjavur.

SHIVA GANGA GARDEN

Located inside the Vijayanagar fort, this beautiful garden is one of the main highlights to explore, apart from the places to visit in Thanjavur other than temples. Amidst the historical ruins of the fort stands this well-designed and well-maintained garden, which is considered to be one of the most famous places in Thanjavur. There is a square tank located inside the Siva Garden, which was built during the 16th century by rulers. This was used to provide water to the Tanjore Palace and is still used while the water tastes sweet.

VIJAYANAGAR FORT

Located at a distance of 2 km, the Vijayanagar Fort is one of the most famous places in Thanjavur. The mesmerizing architecture of the fort was built in 1550 AD by the Nayaks and the Maratha’s. Inside the fort, there is a Tanjore Palace, Sangeetha Mahal, an art gallery, and a library. Alongside this, the Shiva Ganga Garden is also situated inside the complex of the fort, which adds a touch of beauty.

SCHWARTZ CHURCH

This is one of the oldest churches in India and was built by the Maratha ruler, Sarfoji II. During his reign, he came across the Prussian Lutheran missionary Rev. Friedrich Schratwz, who later became his disciple. In order to pay tribute to their friendship, the Maratha ruler decided to construct this around the year 1779.

SWAMI MALAI TEMPLE

This magnificent temple has importance among the Hindu people of South India. Also, this temple is one of the Arupadaiveedu, which is one of the six abodes of Murugan. It is believed that the temple was built over the ground where Lord Murugan imbibed and spread the knowledge of the universe to his father.

SARASWATHI MAHAL LIBRARY

According to the Britannica Encyclopedia, the Saraswathi Mahal Library is considered to be one of the most remarkable libraries in India and has been there for many ages. Built around the 16th and 17th centuries by the Nayak rulers, this place was later nurtured by the Maratha king Serforji II. Inside the library, there is a vast collection of art and maps. Paintings and manuscripts.

THANJAVUR ROYAL PALACE

The complex currently houses the royal family but has a few other interesting buildings, including the fabulous queen’s courtyard, Sadar Mahal Palace, and Durbar Hall. Being a memorial, it contains a gallery where the splendid collection of Chola bronzes can be found. What can we say about one of the top royal places to visit in Thanjavur?

PUNNAI NALLUR MARIAMMAN TEMPLE

The Punnai Nallur Mariamman temple is one of the most famous temples in Tamil Nadu. Dedicated to the goddess Mariamman, the temple is one of the greatest architectural splendors, showcasing the true form of Dravidian style. The temple might be ancient, but the recently built seventh-tier gopuram is still maintained by the royal family of Thanjavur.

THANJAVUR ART GALLERY

There is no doubt why the art gallery in Thanjavur is on the list of the best places to visit in Thanjavur. The world-famous signatures of Thanjavur are the legendary Tanjore paintings and Tanjore dolls. The art gallery is where you will find authentic paintings of Tanjore for sale. You can witness the amazing art pieces on display in the gallery—simply colorful, elegant, and grand.
