About Murudeshwara

Murudeshwara - Second Largest Shiva Statue

Murudeshwar is a beautiful beachside pilgrim town in the Indian southern state of Karnataka. Murudeshwar, which is the other name of the Hindu god Shiva, truly lives up to its name and has the world’s second-tallest statue of Lord Shiva. Located alongside the Arabian Sea, there are numerous beautiful places to visit in Murudeshwar, owing to the fact that this seaside town attracts tourists from far and near.

Popular Temples In Murudeshwara

The town has a divine feel to it. The temples are tranquil, and the beaches serene. Here are all the best places to visit in Murudeshwar. This town surely has plenty to offer for every kind of traveller. There is something for everyone in this divine place. Take a look at the list mentioned below and mark your favourites.

MURUDESHWAR TEMPLE

Located in Karnataka, this magnificent shrine is dedicated to Lord Shiva. This temple boasts the second-highest statue of Lord Shiva in the world, and the sheer magnanimity of the statue will fill you with a sense of awe. This temple is built on Kanduka Hill, which is surrounded by the Arabian Sea on three sides. The main temple's entrance, also called the 'gopura', stands at 123 feet, and you can witness the breathtaking view of the Shiva statue from the top of the gopura. The entire surface of this temple is covered in the most intricate and detailed carvings that you will ever see. The compound of the temple has been modernized except for the sanctum sanctorum, which retains its original form. The main deity of the temple is the Sri Mridesa Linga, which is believed to be a part of the original Atma Linga. The Shiva statue here is quite an impressive one and will surely leave you with a humble sense of tranquility. This magnificent statue is a perfect tribute to a God who is held in high reverence in lands far and wide.

NETRANI ISLAND

Also known as the 'Pigeon Island', Netrani Island in Murudeshwar is located off the coast of Karnataka. Views from above give this island the appearance of being heart-shaped. Rising above the tranquil and azure waters of the Arabian Sea, this heart-shaped island is counted as one of the best sites for scuba diving. This island is a famous tourist spot and a popular pilgrimage town, with silvery sand and the western ghats as a backdrop. Situated about 10 nautical miles away from the holy city of Murudeshwar is the uninhabited island of Netrani, locally known as Netragudo. A thrilling boat ride of 70–90 minutes will take you to Netrani Island from Murudeshwar. Known for its overwhelming experience of underwater world, you will get a chance to come face-to-face with diverse variety of fish life common to Arabian Sea, as well as other varieties such as Napolean Wrasse, Cobia, Stonefish, Black Tip Sharks, Great Barracuda, Turtles stingrays, etc. With a soothing effect on your nerves, this expedition under water is perfect for diving and suitable for certified divers of all experience levels.

MURUDESHWAR BEACH

Lying along the periphery of the majestic Murudeshwar Temple is the beautiful beach by the same name. Overlooking the monumental statue of Lord Shiva, this beach is the perfect picnic spot for families and loved ones. The sand on this beach is quite excellent, and its silky texture is not very common. You can also enjoy boat rides around the magnificent Murudeshwar Temple. Murudeshwar Beach is a major tourist attraction in Karnataka. Surrounded by gentle hills and lush green foliage, this beach is always bustling with activity. The beauty of this place is enhanced by the coconut trees, which are indigenous to this location. Early morning hours are the best time to visit this place, when you might even find the company of flying seagulls and kingfisher birds. With its mighty sea waters splashing against the surface of the beach, the overall scenery of this destination is worthy of a visit.

MURUDESHWAR FORT

Behind the temple complex of Murudeshwar is a fort that finds its origins in the era of the Vijayanagara Kings. It is said to have been renovated after that by none other than Tipu Sultan.

STATUE PARK

A beautiful stretch of green lawns, this area around the 15-meter-tall statue of Shiva houses various flowering plants, stone sculptures, and small ponds inhabited by ducks. The artificial waterfall rushing down over the boulders is a splendid view.

BHATKAL BEACH

A prominent beach situated along the Arabian Sea, with a pristine coastline interspersed with coconut trees, provides a breathtaking view of the surroundings.

MURUDESHWAR MARKET

Being a famous temple town and tourist spot, you can find many shopping options here in Murudeshwar. The temple road of this city is quite famous for its goods, especially souvenirs. You can find various products here that can be put to use as decorative items. The idols and wall hangings here are quite beautiful, and you must consider buying the same. Murudeshwar is an unlikely destination to find some unique handicraft items. Ranging from pen stands to jewelry boxes, you can find quite a few unique pieces here. And if you do end up buying a jewelry box, consider getting some indigenous jewelry from here as well. Authentic in its appearance, it will add another dimension to your jewelry collection. Don't forget to try out the local delicacies here, which are a lip-smacking experience.
